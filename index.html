Welcome! I'm a PhD student in Economics at the University of Michigan.

My research interests are in public finance, with a focus on optimal taxation. I combine theory and econometric methods to design tax policies that address real-world complexities, such as tax evasion and large informal economies.

You can contact me at apfranco@umich.edu.

Research Papers

Payments Under the Table: Estimation and Implications for Tax Policy Job Market Paper [Link]

Abstract
I develop a model where firms and workers may underreport wages (PUT). A corporate tax alters the classic optimal income tax formula via a fiscal externality and by lowering the elasticity of taxable income when it discourages PUT. Using matched administrative and survey data from Peru, I show PUT is common and declines when the corporate tax rises.

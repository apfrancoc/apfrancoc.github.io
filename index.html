Payments Under the Table and Optimal Income Taxation

Paper Slides Appendix
Abstract
I develop a model where firms and workers may underreport wages (PUT). A corporate tax changes the classic optimal income tax formula through a fiscal externality and by reducing the elasticity of taxable income when it discourages PUT. Using matched administrative and survey data from Peru, I show PUT is common and decreases when the corporate tax rises.
